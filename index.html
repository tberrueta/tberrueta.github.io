About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate and Presidential Fellow at the Northwestern University Center for Robotics and Biosystems. I am a member of the Interactive and Emergent Autonomy Laboratory, and am advised by Dr. Todd D. Murphey. I received my B.S. in Engineering from Harvey Mudd College in 2017 and my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Northwestern University in 2020.

I am an interdisciplinary scientist and engineer focusing on understanding and controlling complex robotic systems. From termite construction to the molecular machinery of cells, emergent behaviors in nature stand as proof that collections of simple elements can become greater than the sum of their parts.
